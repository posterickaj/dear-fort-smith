Dear Fort Smith,

In 1956, ArDot's Chief Engineer told us not to follow the advice of Fort Smith's Chief Engineer.

A. Johnson rejected the lazy math of roadway expansion. One does not simply take today’s traffic, assume it will grow by whatever percent every year, and then build the road suitable for whatever number you land on.

Which is exactly what the city has done to justify widening Massard Road, which is where the leftmost quote is from. 

 We looked at a few years of unusually high growth, assumed it will continue, and then talked ourselves into expanding a road to nowhere.

The better path is incremental growth. Play "money ball" with streets dollars by humbly observing where people are struggling most, and make small, reversible improvements there. If one of our adjustments works, build upon it. If something doesn't work, claw it back. Large, transformative expansions like Massard Road aren't an investment, they are a gamble. We should not gamble with public tax dollars.
