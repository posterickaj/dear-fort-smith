Dear Fort Smith,

This is you, if we remove everything except your parking lots.

I have heard parking described as everything from "valuable" to "rare." What is not mentioned or considered is that parking is land, infrastructure, and opportunity. This makes it central to public finance issues.

Every red shape on this map is land that has to be reached by police, fire, water, sewer, drainage, and roads. It has to be driven past. It has to be lit, drained, patrolled, and maintained around. Even when the parking lot is empty, the city still has to serve the pattern of development that created it.

That does not mean parking is bad. Cities need parking. Businesses need customers. People need access.

The issue is not whether parking should exist. The issue is whether the city should legally require so much of it, subsidize the infrastructure around it, and then act surprised when the math does not work.

A productive city gets a lot of value out of a small amount of land. A fragile city spreads everything out, then asks taxpayers and utility customers to maintain the distance between things.

Parking mandates make that worse.

Ending parking mandates would not ban parking, but rather would stop the city from forcing every property owner to overbuy.

There is an odd refrain that cost of free parking is baked in to the cost of goods and services that we all enjoy. That Wal-Mart's parking lot maintenance is paid for by Wal-Mart, and that a portion of our purchases goes to maintain the lot. That is true. But there is no such accounting for the length of pipe that the public must maintain to get to the other side of that lot. If pipes last 80-100 years, and we are experiencing an infrastructure crisis from 80 year old pipes that we cannot afford to maintain, that means 90,000 of us can't afford to maintain the set of pipes built in 1946, when Fort Smith was half its size. 

What do you expect our children to do 80 years from now, when the current pipes come due for replacement?

The price of parking is not baked into the price of goods. It is on the credit card, to be paid by our grandchildren.
