Most U.S. building codes require two full staircases in apartments buildings above three stories. Two exceptions are New York City, where single-stair buildings are common (they used fire escapes instead, until 1968), and Seattle (where developers can build single-stair apartments if they include additional safety measures).

On a narrow lot, that means single-stair buildings free up 7% of floor area and 6-13% of construction costs. Both of these increase the supply of housing and, just, like, allow for cuter buildings.
