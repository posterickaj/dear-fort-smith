When you go to work every day, your labor creates value:

\* Producing Goods
\* Providing Services
* Solving Problems

Part of that value returns to you as wages.

Another part goes to your employer.

The third part is the value you create that goes to nearby landowners.

You and your employer both attract foot traffic, infrastructure, and economic activity to the surrounding area of your workplace, which creates LAND VALUE.

Your labor creates wealth for the guy who owns the vacant lot nextdoor to your work.

This involuntary redistribution of wealth is an unseen tax that we ALL pay, and because of it, land, which existed before any of us even got here, accounts for one third of global wealth.

The single largest source of wealth is completely unearned.
